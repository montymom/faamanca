Innsbruck: What to do when you get there?

Innsbruck is a city located in western Austria, in the Inn valley in the heart of the Alps. As a famous winter sports centre, it hosted twice the Winter Olympics. Thanks to its perfect location, you can practice plenty of outdoor activities and extreme sports.

Open to the world and dynamic, the city of Innsbruck has successfully preserved its culture and traditions. It is also a vibrant city with numerous festivals which take place all year round. Get the most out of your trip and don't miss visiting Innsbruck's treasures, such as the Imperial Palace, the Tirol Panorama, the Imperial Church, the Castle of Ambras and many more.

Whatever the season, you can always enjoy amazing outdoor activities, whether it's hiking, rock climbing, mountain biking, freeriding, snowboarding, skiing or even rafting.

Winter sports are obviously very popular in Innsbruck, but if you are a bit sensitive to the cold, hiking and mountain biking in spring or summer is an excellent way to explore the wonderful landscapes of the Austrian Alps, including the Karwendel Natural Park. 

If you wish to get thrills on the water, you'd better try canyoning or rafting in Innsbruck's surroundings. You can even combine both activities. The experienced guides will take you to different spots, starting with a rafting excursion. Raft down the Inn, the most powerful river in the Tyrolean Alps. Here, impressing waves and huge waterfalls await you on your way from the gigantic Imster Gorge to Imst and Haiming.

Then, get ready for a fascinating canyoning excursion in the Hachlschlucht near Hoch Imst. Equipped with rope, climbing harness and helmet, you will explore the gorge. You are guaranteed pure fun while experiencing a wide variety of abseiling, an outdoor dream!

For additional thrills, dare to jump from the Europa bridge, at 192 metres from the ground! A crazy bungee jumping located only 5 km south from Innsbruck, on the Brenner motorway.

Innsbruck is the ideal spot to practice a plethora of outdoor activities and extreme sports while enjoying breathtaking landscapes.
